A knowledge graph is a structured representation of facts and relationships between entities.

Nodes (Entities): People, places, objects, concepts.
Edges (Relationships): How entities are connected (e.g., "Paris is the capital of France").

Why use Knowledge Graphs?
Improve search (Google Knowledge Graph).
Enable reasoning & inference.

LLMs & Knowledge Graphs

🔹 Large Language Models (LLMs) → AI trained on vast text datasets to generate and understand language (e.g., chatGPT, Gemini).

🔹 Knowledge Graphs (KGs) → Structured databases representing facts, entities, and relationships.

🔹 Why combine LLMs & KGs?

LLMs are good at generalizing from text but may generate hallucinations.
KGs provide structured, factual knowledge but lack reasoning capabilities.
Together, they improve accuracy, reasoning, and interpretability in AI.
